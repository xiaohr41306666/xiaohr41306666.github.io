Project Overview

Location: Australia

Our main exploration area is the Great Barrier Reef on Australia's northeast coast - the world's largest and longest coral reef system, making it the perfect location for our conservation activities.

Core Objectives

Through volunteer participation, we conduct diving training, field sampling, research interviews, and scientific studies to reveal the causes of coral loss and promote the importance of coral conservation.

Expected Outcomes

Summarize experimental results, explain reasons for coral degradation, promote coral importance, and conduct outreach through QR codes, mini-programs, and WeChat public account links.

Why Coral Reefs Matter

Marine Biodiversity

Coral reefs are the "rainforests of the sea," providing habitats for 25% of global marine species and supporting the balance of complex marine ecosystems.

Coastal Protection

Coral reefs effectively buffer wave impacts, reduce coastal erosion, and lower the risk of damage from natural disasters such as tsunamis and storm surges.

Urgent Need for Conservation

Field research shows that local residents have insufficient awareness of coral ecological value, and coral degradation has affected fishery resources and tourism economy.

Project Itinerary

Diving Training (3-5 days)

Ensure volunteers master safe diving skills to reach Australian coral breeding grounds. Training covers theoretical knowledge, underwater breathing techniques, equipment use, and emergency response.

Note: If you don't have professional diving certificates, we provide short-term training (additional fee applies).

Field Sampling & Research (1 week)

Coral Site Sampling (4 days): Collect coral samples to document growth status and distribution.

Field Interviews (3 days): Conduct surveys with local residents to understand awareness of coral reef issues.

Scientific Research (1 week)

Conduct specialized research in biology and chemistry on coral loss, decay, and population decline. Analyze key factors affecting coral survival with support from professional laboratories and expert research teams.

Research Results

Coral Loss Causes

Experimental results show that coral loss is mainly related to rising ocean temperatures, ocean acidification, and overfishing, which disrupt the coral's living environment and food chain balance.

Coral Decay Mechanisms

Research found that coral decay is mostly caused by bacterial infections and algal competition; when coral immunity declines, it becomes vulnerable to pathogen invasion, leading to tissue necrosis.

Coral Population Decline

Comprehensive analysis indicates that human activities (coastal development, pollution) and climate change are core causes of sharp coral population decline, requiring targeted conservation strategies.
